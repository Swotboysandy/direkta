# DIREKTA — Story Bible (The Storybook)
### Format & Structure Reference

---

## What Is the Storybook?

The Storybook is **Direkta's signature deliverable** — a new format that doesn't exist in traditional filmmaking. It is not a document. It is not a folder of files. It is a **designed, visual, living artifact** that tells the story of the project in the same way the film will tell its story — through image, atmosphere, and emotion.

Where the Movie Bible is a reference tool for crew and investors, the Storybook is a **creative object** — something a director would keep, show, and feel proud of. Every spread combines text and image. Every section breathes.

Think: *a coffee table book about a film that doesn't exist yet.*

---

## Philosophy

Traditional film documents are **functional**. The Storybook is **expressive**.

It takes everything Direkta generates — logline, outline, characters, scenes, storyboards, mood boards — and assembles them into a single visual narrative. The reader experiences the project emotionally before a single frame is shot.

**Primary uses:**
- Pitching to producers, investors, distributors
- Onboarding department heads (DP, costume, production design)
- Personal creative reference for the director
- Social content — shareable pages from the Storybook as marketing

---

## Structure

### SPREAD 01 — THE COVER
- Project title in full typography treatment
- A single hero image (AI-generated, representing the world or a key moment)
- Tagline or logline beneath
- No clutter. Maximum impact.

---

### SPREAD 02 — THE HEARTBEAT
*What is this film, in its soul?*

- The logline
- A 2-3 sentence director's note — why this story, why now, what it means to them
- 3 words that describe the film's emotional register
- A full-bleed atmospheric image behind the text

---

### SPREAD 03 — THE WORLD
*Where and when does this live?*

- Location + time period in large type
- Atmosphere paragraph — sensory, poetic, present tense
- 4-6 world reference images in a designed grid
- Colour palette swatches pulled from the images
- Sound/music direction note

---

### SPREAD 04 — THE STORY
*What happens?*

- One-liner (all scenes, one page)
- Three-act structure visualised as a timeline with emotional beats marked
- Short synopsis beneath — 2 paragraphs
- Transition images between acts

---

### SPREAD 05 — THE CHARACTERS
*Who lives in this world?*

One sub-spread per major character:

- Character name in display type
- AI-generated portrait (consistent style across all characters)
- 3-line psychology summary — want / fear / wound
- Arc described as a visual journey (start → break → end)
- Wardrobe swatch
- Key quote from the script (one line of dialogue that defines them)
- Relationship map (simple visual showing how they connect to others)

---

### SPREAD 06 — THE SCENES
*How does the story move?*

One sub-spread per act (or per major sequence):

- Scene range (e.g. Scenes 1-14 / Act One)
- Storyboard panels for key shots in this sequence
- Shot list summary (condensed)
- Emotional register of this section
- Transition idea to next act (cut, dissolve, match cut, time jump)
- Atmospheric image representing the sequence

---

### SPREAD 07 — THE VISUAL LANGUAGE
*How will this look?*

- Full mood board (12 images, curated grid)
- Cinematography notes — lens, movement, distance
- Lighting direction — diagrams or reference images
- Colour grade reference — before/after style stills
- Editorial rhythm — pace notes, transition style
- 3 key visual motifs that recur through the film

---

### SPREAD 08 — THE DETAILS
*What makes up the world?*

- Props spread — key props with images and scene context
- Wardrobe spread — per character, per act
- Location spread — each major location with reference imagery and atmosphere notes
- Sound design notes — what the world sounds like

---

### SPREAD 09 — THE PITCH
*Why this, why now, why us?*

- Comparable films with brief positioning notes
- Target audience + market context (brief)
- What makes this unique — one strong paragraph
- Production tier and format
- Team / director bio (brief)
- Call to action

---

### SPREAD 10 — THE APPENDIX
*Everything else, linked.*

- Full screenplay (link)
- Full shot list (link)
- Full storyboard sequence (link)
- Full Movie Bible (link)
- Casting breakdown (link)
- Budget summary (link, optional)

---

## Direkta-Specific Rules

- The Storybook is **assembled automatically** from everything Direkta generates — the user doesn't lay it out manually
- Every image is **AI-generated within the project's consistent visual context** — characters look the same across every spread
- The Storybook is **exportable as PDF** for pitching and **shareable as a link** for online viewing
- Individual spreads are **exportable as images** for social/marketing use
- The Storybook **updates automatically** when the underlying script or Bible changes
- The aesthetic of the Storybook **adapts to the film's tone** — a horror Storybook looks different from a romantic drama Storybook

---

## The Difference: Bible vs Storybook

| | Movie Bible | Storybook |
|---|---|---|
| **Purpose** | Reference & production tool | Pitch & emotional artifact |
| **Audience** | Crew, line producers, investors | Producers, investors, directors, fans |
| **Format** | Structured document | Designed visual spreads |
| **Tone** | Functional | Expressive |
| **Images** | Reference images | Full visual spreads |
| **Export** | PDF / structured data | PDF / shareable link / social images |
| **Updates** | When script changes | When script changes |
| **Uniqueness** | Industry standard format | Direkta original format |
