Break the script into numbered beats in order. Each beat is one tight, visual paragraph — a decision the camera can see, not exposition or backstory. For each beat capture the scene heading, the characters present, the mood, and the key props. Preserve the writer's voice and intent; never invent characters, locations, or events the script does not establish. Flag any continuity risk you notice rather than silently resolving it.
