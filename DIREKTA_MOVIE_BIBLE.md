# DIREKTA — Movie Bible
### Format & Structure Reference

---

## What Is a Movie Bible?

A Movie Bible is the **definitive reference document for a single film**. It captures everything a director, producer, cinematographer, or investor needs to understand the project — story, characters, world, tone, and visual language — in one place.

It is created once per project and lives at the top of the Direkta workspace. Every other document (shot list, storyboard, casting breakdown) is downstream from it.

---

## Structure

### 1. TITLE PAGE
- Project title
- Format (Feature / Short / Series)
- Genre
- Logline (one sentence)
- Director name
- Draft date / version number

---

### 2. LOGLINE
One sentence. Protagonist + conflict + stakes.

> *A burned-out detective in near-future Mumbai must solve a murder that implicates the AI system running the city — before it erases the evidence, and him.*

---

### 3. SYNOPSIS
- **Short synopsis** — 1 paragraph (for pitch decks, one-pagers)
- **Full synopsis** — 3-5 paragraphs, act by act (beginning, midpoint, end)

No dialogue. Written in present tense. Narrative tone matching the film.

---

### 4. TONE & THEMES
- **Tone** — 2-3 sentences describing emotional register. Is it bleak? Hopeful? Tense? Darkly comic?
- **Core themes** — 3-5 thematic ideas explored (identity, power, grief, etc.)
- **Comparable films** — 2-3 reference titles with brief notes on what Direkta shares and doesn't share with each
- **What makes this different** — one paragraph on the unique angle

---

### 5. WORLD / SETTING
- **Time period** — when does this take place?
- **Location(s)** — where, described in texture and atmosphere, not just geography
- **World rules** — what is true in this world that isn't true in ours? (For genre films)
- **Atmosphere** — sensory description: light, sound, smell, weather, pace of life
- **Visual references** — mood images attached (photographers, paintings, films, locations)

---

### 6. CHARACTERS

Each character gets their own spread:

#### [Character Name]
- **Role** — protagonist / antagonist / supporting
- **Age & appearance** — brief physical description
- **Background** — relevant history in 2-3 sentences
- **Psychology** — what drives them, what they fear, what they want vs. what they need
- **Arc** — where they start, what breaks them open, where they end
- **Voice** — how they speak (terse, verbose, formal, poetic, deflecting)
- **Key relationships** — how they relate to other main characters
- **Visual reference** — AI-generated portrait or reference image
- **Wardrobe direction** — palette, silhouette, evolution across the story

---

### 7. VISUAL LANGUAGE

- **Cinematography direction** — lens choices, camera movement philosophy, distance from subjects
- **Colour palette** — primary palette with hex codes and reference stills
- **Lighting philosophy** — hard/soft, natural/artificial, time-of-day preferences
- **Editorial rhythm** — cutting pace, transition style, use of silence
- **Visual references** — specific films, photographers, paintings
- **Mood board** — 6-12 images representing the visual world

---

### 8. SCENE BREAKDOWN SUMMARY
A compressed overview of every scene in the script:
- Scene number
- INT/EXT
- Location
- Time of day
- Characters present
- One-line description
- Emotional register
- Key props/wardrobe/SFX flags

---

### 9. PRODUCTION NOTES
- Budget tier (micro / indie / mid / studio)
- Key production challenges
- VFX requirements summary
- Special equipment needs
- Casting direction (not names — archetypes, qualities, approach)

---

### 10. ATTACHMENTS
- Full screenplay (linked)
- Shot list (linked, generated)
- Storyboards (linked, generated)
- Casting breakdown (linked, generated)
- The Storybook (linked — see Storybook spec)

---

## Direkta-Specific Rules

- The Bible is **generated from the script** — the user doesn't fill it in manually
- Every image reference is **AI-generated or user-uploaded**, never pulled from the web
- All character descriptions feed the **image generation pipeline** for consistency
- The Bible is the **single source of truth** — if it changes, downstream docs regenerate
- Version history is tracked — every draft is saved
