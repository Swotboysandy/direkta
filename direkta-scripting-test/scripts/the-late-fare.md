THE LATE FARE
A short.

---

INT. TAXI — NIGHT

Rain. A windshield smeared with neon. NADIA (behind the wheel, watchful,
a paper coffee cup going cold in the holder) drives an empty street.

The dispatch crackles. A pin drops on her dash screen. She sighs, turns.

A man climbs in fast — VICTOR, soaked, a soft leather case clutched to his
chest like it's breathing. He doesn't give an address. He just says:

                         VICTOR
              Drive. Anywhere that isn't here.

Nadia watches him in the mirror. She pulls out.

                         NADIA
              Meter's running either way.

                         VICTOR
              Good. Keep it running.

He looks back through the rear window. Something back there. We don't see what.

---

EXT. RIVER BRIDGE — NIGHT

The taxi slows mid-span. Empty. Wind off the water. Victor stares at the
black river below.

                         VICTOR
              You ever carry something you wish
              you'd never picked up?

Nadia says nothing. Her hands tighten on the wheel.

                         VICTOR (CONT'D)
              Stop here.

                         NADIA
              You don't want to stop here.

He's already opening the door.

---

INT. TAXI — CONTINUOUS

The passenger door hangs open. Victor is gone. The leather case sits on the
back seat, exactly where he was.

Nadia looks at it in the mirror for a long moment. Rain fills the silence.

She reaches back. Pulls it into the front. Sets it on the passenger seat.

She does not open it. She puts the car in gear.

                         NADIA
                       (to herself)
              Keep it running.

She drives. The neon swallows the taxi.

FADE OUT.
