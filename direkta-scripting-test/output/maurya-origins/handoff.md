## 1 · Look-lock summary

Historical epic, c. 320–300 BCE Magadha (D2), shot in **Hollywood prestige-epic grammar, Game-of-Thrones-esque** (D3) — desaturated, naturalistic single-source light, *never* the saturated Indian-TV mythological idiom. **Lighting law from the page:** tyranny scenes are DAY (pitiless public sun: S2, S3, S4), conspiracy scenes are NIGHT (fire and candle: S6, S8); the trailer travels black → dawn, and the S9 sunrise must be the first clean warm light in the piece. Power frames are static and symmetrical; the camera goes handheld only where the page moves (S5 chase, S7 montage). Tempo: slow dread → build → full stop at S8 (the cup hangs in silence) → surge → final line over black.

## 2 · Cast identity descriptors (verbatim contract — hold these in every frame)

- **CHANDRAGUPTA — boy (~12–14 · S2, S5, S6, early S7):** lean, wiry, hunger-hardened street kid; dark unkempt hair; dust-darkened skin; the defining feature is the level gaze that does not drop when every other head bows. Threadbare, dust-toned market-poor dress.
- **CHANDRAGUPTA — man (~20s · late S7, S9, S10):** the same level gaze in a soldier's frame — sun-weathered, economical, instinct-fast; simple warrior's dress of leather, iron, undyed cloth; no ornament, **never gold**. Feature-matched to the boy (D5) — one person, two ages; the jump lives inside the S7 montage (F5).
- **CHANAKYA (prime · S3–S10):** gaunt, sinewy scholar; hawk-sharp unblinking eyes; spine as straight as his staff even when bleeding; austere undyed cloth against the court's gold; carries scrolls or a staff, never a sword. **Hair LOOSE in S3; tied in the oath-knot at S4 and knotted in every frame after (F3).** Blood at lip in S4 only (F4). Aged version is voice-only, third-person narrator (D7) — never on camera.
- **DHANA NANDA:** a king of soft excess draped in gold — ringed fingers, oiled beard, heavy-lidded amusement that ices over when pierced; always seated, always above, the golden cup never far from his hand.

## 3 · Palette law

**Gold = the regime** (court scenes and regime bodies ONLY — gold never touches a rebel) · **dust = the people** (greys, browns, grain tones) · **ember = the rebellion** (fire-orange accents: village fire S1 → teaching fire S6 → forge S7) · red exactly once (Chanakya's lip, S4) · black → dawn across the whole piece.

## 4 · Motifs to keep paying

1. **The unlowered gaze** — extreme close-ups on eyes; the page scripts them (S2, S4, S10).
2. **Fire changing ownership** — S1 tyranny's, S6 teaching's, S7 rebellion's.
3. **Falling and rising** — bodies go down, eyes and bodies come back up (S2, S4, S7).
4. **The arrested object** — the golden cup stopped halfway (S8): power interrupted mid-gesture.
5. **The oath-knot** — Chanakya's vow worn on his head from S4 to the end.

## 5 · Frame
