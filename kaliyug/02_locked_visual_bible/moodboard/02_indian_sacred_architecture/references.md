# 02 · Indian Sacred Architecture

The indigenous Hindu sacred layer (oldest/deepest stratum). Grounded stone — **no floating geometry here** (that's dreams only).

## Pulls
- **Ellora — Kailasa temple (Cave 16)** — colossal **monolithic rock-cut** shrine carved top-down from one cliff; the deepest, oldest, purest stone (the shattered-shrine sanctum). https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kailasa_Temple,_Ellora
- **Brihadeeswarar (Thanjavur) & Meenakshi (Madurai)** — Dravidian **vimana** tower + towering carved **gopuram** gateways. https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brihadeeswarar_Temple
- **Khajuraho / Konark** — Nagara **shikhara** spire; obsessive **carving density** (the wall registers to deface/corrupt).
- **Jali screens** (Fatehpur Sikri, Humayun's Tomb, Sidi Saiyyed) — pierced stone lattice = KALIYUG's signature light-tool. https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jaali
- **Stepped tanks, mandapa pillar-halls, garbhagriha sanctums.**

## What to pull for KALIYUG
- **shikhara** (N, curved) vs **vimana + gopuram** (S, stepped) — keep them distinguishable.
- **jali** in three states: intact (god-light shafts through ash) / broken (black filigree) / corrupted (veins weeping ichor).
- **Carving density = meaning density** — intact = remembered dharma; defaced = "meaning gone"; corrupted = faces overgrown by flesh-growths, broken dharma-wheels.
- **Rock-cut monolithic scale** for the deepest sacred set-pieces.
