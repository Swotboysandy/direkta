## 03 · Synopsis

### SHORT

The last age of the cycle, Kali Yuga, has rotted itself to collapse: greed became intelligence, power became morality, and World War III left Bharat a poisoned ruin of ash, rust, and red-black corruption crawling over fallen temples and dead modern infrastructure. Hidden near Mount Kailash, a divinely preserved sanctuary kept one untouched cradle of clean rivers and living forest — and there a child was born of a virgin birth, his mother dying as he drew breath, raised in isolation by an ancient watchful crow named Kaka. He grows up uncorrupted but unaware that he is Kalki. When the crow finally leads him out of paradise into the ruined human world, the seeker confronts greed, tyranny, and Kali's systemic rot firsthand, receives fragmentary visions in a cosmic dream realm (the Pathways), and slowly gathers proof of a destiny he resists. Awakening late and at great cost, he claims the divine bow Pinaka from the serpent Vasuki, manifests the Brahma Kavach aura and the Sharanga chakra-shield, and turns hope into war against Kali and the late-age threat of Raavan — choosing, consciously and grievingly, to be the force that ends the age.

### FULL

**Phase 1 — Innocence in Sanctuary.** Reality turns through repeating yuga cycles, and the final age has reached its collapse. Humanity decayed from within long before the bombs — values rotted first, and Kali, the demon-personification of the age, spread not as a horned monster but as a force embedded in systems, incentives, rulers, and human weakness. World War III is only the visible consequence: poisoned skies, burning zones, mutated ecosystems, machine ruins mistaken for temples. Hidden in the high Himalaya near Mount Kailash, a sanctuary survives by design — pure rivers, fertile soil, unmutated life, a contingency cradle for the next cycle. There a child is born through mysterious means, no father, no public prophecy; his mother dies in childbirth, leaving only the infant and silence. A crow, Kaka, watches over him and raises him. The boy grows with divine intellect but no society, learning directly from nature — pattern, instinct, silence, cause and effect — emotionally uncorrupted but socially unformed. Neither he nor the audience is told he is Kalki.

**Phase 2 — Entry into Ruined Humanity.** When the boy can survive alone, Kaka leads him out of the sanctuary — the symbolic fall from innocence into history. The outside world shocks him: people kill for scraps, leaders exploit suffering, religion is commercialized, technology survives without wisdom, worship continues but meaning is gone. He meets monsters and saints. He sees civilization with alien clarity. He must understand humanity before he can judge whether it deserves saving. Through this passage Kali's hand is everywhere — a diseased-gold bazaar of debt and dice, an empty black-and-gold throne that warlords and corrupt priests kneel before though no body sits in it. Kali does not need to conquer the world; he taught it to kneel.

**Phase 3 — Search for Truth.** The seeker begins receiving dreams that are not passive symbolism but interactive cosmic spaces — endless temples of stone and light, floating sacred geometry, oceans of stars, impossible architecture (this divine imagery exists only here, never in the grounded ruined world). Guides appear — sages, warriors, strangers, ordinary people — and speak only in fragments, because he must discover truth rather than be handed doctrine. He is spiritual by nature but rational by temperament: he tries to read miracles as systems, visions as signals, fate as pattern. He demonstrates impossible traits — heightened reflexes, unnatural resilience, instinctive combat mastery, resistance to corruption, reactions to sacred objects — and others begin to suspect what he is before he does. Near a shattered shrine, a cracked relic answers him: blue-white light pulses and the corruption recoils like burned tissue. Across the ruins, Kali feels it and is afraid — recognition. The dream realm opens the **Pathways** — six cosmic dream sequences, in each of which Kalki meets a god (some paired consorts) who helps him realize his destiny along a ladder of Grace, Duty, Knowledge, Self-realisation, and Perception; Lakshmi opens it, and the sixth — **Vishnu** (Acceptance) — is the culmination he must *become*.

**Phase 4 — Awakening.** The truth converges: who he is, why he was hidden, why the world fell, why Kali fears him, why power without wisdom caused the collapse. Vishnu's deeper design is revealed in its meaning — Kalki was kept ignorant so that he could know suffering, doubt, temptation, and love firsthand, so that his judgment of humanity would be legitimate rather than mere programming. Awakening does not make him arrogant; it burdens him. To become Kalki is to surrender any ordinary life. The prophecy's cruelest demand is that he *choose* it. Around this turn, Kaka's true identity is finally revealed — the crow is Kakabhushundi, a human in crow form, the ancient witness who has guided him in fragments all along. Kalki descends into a cavern-shrine of living rock where Vasuki, Lord Shiva's vast serpent, has guarded the bow Pinaka since the first age. The serpent tests him in wordless judgment; Kalki does not flinch; the coils part, and his hand closes on Pinaka — light and ash, rippling between bow, spear, and blade, never metal.

**Phase 5 — War Against Kali.** On an infected battlefield, the Brahma Kavach ignites as a luminous armor-aura woven to his frame, the Sharanga burns into being as a spinning chakra-shield, and Pinaka draws as a bow of pure light; the red veins rip backward, shrieking. But the war is order against entropy, truth against manipulation, consciousness against compulsion — Kali must be defeated in the world *and* within humanity, and within Kalki himself. Survivors rise from temple settlements; hope learns to make war. As the age sets a crown on its own head, Kalki drives toward Kali's massing host, clearing a single narrow road of corruption behind him. On a ridge above a dead ash plain he raises Pinaka against the red sky and chooses: if this age must end, let it end through him. And far behind the burning fortresses, a colossal demon-emperor — Raavan — opens ember-red eyes, a late and final threat of the age, and looks down. Kali whispers: *not yet.* The saga moves toward the final confrontation, the collapse of the corrupted age, the end of Kali Yuga, and the renewal of the cycle — whether Kali is destroyed and the cycle resets, contained under eternal vigilance, or humanity is left to choose its own future as Kalki passes back into legend.

## 04 · Tone & Themes

**Tone.** Mysterious, epic, philosophical, and emotionally grounded — mythological scale carried through psychological realism. This is *Dune*-weight destiny, not spectacle: every truth arrives in fragments, every answer raises a larger question, and the supernatural is left ambiguous enough that the audience wonders whether a miracle is divine or simply beyond understanding. The world is desolate and frightening; awakening is a burden, not a thrill.

**Core themes.**
- **Kali vs. Kalki (the underlying spine)** — the demon of the age against the final avatar; every other theme hangs on this central conflict.
- **Destiny vs. free will** — Kalki is the *required* balancing force at the end of the age, yet must choose the burden consciously; a destined corrective, never an accident.
- **Corruption of civilization** — values rotted before the bombs; Kali is systemic decay, not a single villain.
- **Power without wisdom** — the named cause of the collapse, and the trap Kalki must not fall into.
- **Inner Kali vs. outer Kali** — corruption must be defeated within humanity (and within Kalki) as much as on the battlefield.
- **Cyclical time** — creation, preservation, destruction, renewal; the Kalki/Kali conflict belongs to the turning of the age.
- **Self-realization and the legitimacy of judgment** — an unaware seeker who must earn the right to judge whether broken humanity deserves saving.
- **Sacrifice and rebirth after collapse** — the green seed pressed into cleared dark soil; restoration that costs the savior his ordinary life.

**Comparable films.**
- ***Dune*** — destiny-weighted fate and a reluctant chosen one who fears what he is, mythic gravity over spectacle.
- ***Apocalypto / The Road*** (post-collapse realism) — grounded, physical ruin and survival, no clean sci-fi sheen.
- ***Princess Mononoke*** — sacred nature against a corrupted, industrialized world; moral complexity over good-vs-evil.
- ***Children of Men*** — grounded miraculous-birth-in-collapse; a reluctant protagonist carrying an impossible hope through a ruined world, ambiguous and burdened, with no clean fantasy sheen.

**What makes this different.** Indian myth used *seriously*, not cosmetically: Kalki is treated as a character with doubt and wound, not a symbol; modern collapse is tied to timeless yuga patterns; spirituality and systems-thinking coexist in a single protagonist who is spiritual by nature but rational by temperament. The grounded real world never borrows the cosmos's imagery — sacred geometry is quarantined to dreams — which keeps the ruin frightening and the divine genuinely otherworldly.

## 05 · World

**Time period.** Post–World War III; the end of Kali Yuga, the final age of the cycle.

**Aspect ratio.** 16:9 cinematic.

**Atmosphere.** A grounded dark-fantasy epic after global war, where Indian sacred architecture, ruined modern civilization, demonic corruption, and spiritual destiny collide. Civilization did not end cleanly — it rotted while still alive. Physical textures rule: ash, cracked stone, rust, mud, blood-stained cloth, bone, soot, burnt wood, dead roots, black ichor, glowing red cracks. The corruption looks like civilization itself has become infected tissue: black-red organic veins crawling up towers, wet ichor, flesh-like growths fused into stone and metal, corrupted temple spires wrapped in red-black tendrils.

**World rules.**
- The **real world** is desolate, post-apocalyptic, dark-magic-infected, red-themed where corrupted, vein-choked. It must NEVER show sacred geometry, floating mandalas, cosmic diagrams, holograms, luminous abstract symbols, or clean metaphysical portals.
- **Sacred geometry, cosmic mandalas, floating symbols, impossible architecture, and metaphysical diagrams belong ONLY to dreams, visions, and spiritual-revelation sequences** — the hidden order behind reality: cosmic, beautiful, overwhelming, dangerous.
- **Kali is NEVER Goddess Kali** — never divine feminine, never multi-armed/tongue-out/Shakti-wrath iconography. Kali is the demon-personification of the age: an asuric shadow intelligence, bodiless and systemic.
- **Divine forms appear only in the dream/vision realm**: male = pinkish electric blue, female = bluish electric pink; formless, translucent, ethereal.
- This is **grounded mythic** — never tech, AI, cyberpunk, simulation, data-space, or multiverse-as-system. Machine ruins are mistaken for temples; technology survives without wisdom but is not the cosmology.

**Grounded-Puranic cosmology note.** Reality runs in repeating **yuga cycles** — creation, preservation, destruction, renewal — and the present is the collapse of the last age. The **Trimurti** is understood as cosmic *function*, not as characters who walk the ruined earth: **Brahma** creates, **Vishnu** preserves continuity and balance, **Shiva** destroys what has failed and enables renewal. Behind them, **Brahman** is the infinite ground of being and **Adi Parashakti** the primordial activating force; souls are evolving consciousnesses growing through karma. **Kali** is the corrupting force of *this* age made manifest. **Kalki** is Vishnu's destined balancing function at the turning — not anomaly, but correction. There is no multiverse-as-system, no simulation, no AI cosmology — only the cycle, the cosmic functions, and the dream realm where they are glimpsed.

## 06 · Characters

### KALKI
**Role.** Protagonist — the unaware seeker who must consciously choose the burden of the final avatar.
**Brief.** Young Indian male; lean, alert, physically capable; ash-grimed skin; rough survival clothing and a torn shawl in the first half — not royal, not superheroic, not armored.
**Key quote.** *"If this age must end... let it end through me."*
**Background.** Born of a virgin birth in the Kailash sanctuary; his mother died at his birth. Raised in total isolation by the crow Kaka, learning from nature rather than society. Possesses divine intellect, heightened reflexes, unnatural resilience, instinctive combat mastery, and resistance to corruption — long before he knows what any of it means.
**Want / Fear / Wound.** *Want:* the single truth that reconciles spiritual and rational explanations of his impossible life. *Fear:* that he is the thing the corruption recognizes — and that accepting it will cost him every ordinary possibility. *Wound:* born into silence, motherless, raised apart; uncorrupted but socially unformed, never having belonged to the humanity he must judge.
**Arc.** *Start:* an analytical seeker who reads miracles as systems and refuses to simply accept prophecy. *Break open:* the shrine-relic answers his hand and the corruption recoils; the Pathways open in dream; the burden converges and he weeps choosing it ("This is not a gift. It is a sentence."). *End:* having claimed Pinaka, donned the Brahma Kavach aura, and turned hope into war, he stands on the ridge and consciously elects to be the force that ends the age — burdened, grieving, resolved.
**Voice.** Measured, watchful, precise; few words; reasons aloud about systems and patterns; lines land like decisions, not boasts.
**Wardrobe.** *First half:* survival cloth, torn shawl, practical wraps, ash; no glow. *Second half:* blue-white divine aura, the Brahma Kavach as luminous armor-aura (not metal), Pinaka as transforming bow/spear/blade of light and ash, Sharanga as chakra-shield — powerful but grounded.
**Relationships.** Raised by Kaka (his first parent, guide, and mystery); hunted and feared by Kali; tested and accepted by Vasuki; instructed in fragments by the Unseen Mentor of the dream realm; narrated and prophesied over by the Old Sage.

### KALI
**Role.** Primary antagonist — the demon/personification of Kali Yuga: corruption, vice, decay, falsehood, gambling, violence, systemic rot. **NEVER Goddess Kali / never divine feminine.**
**Brief.** Bodiless and systemic — an asuric shadow intelligence. Manifests as an empty throne, falling dice, red-black smoke that moves like thought, layered whispers through static; demonic male or abstract monstrous forms only when suggested, never a horned monster-king.
**Key quote.** *"Fear is a coin. Desire... is a leash."*
**Background.** The corrupting force of the age, spread through minds, institutions, incentives, media, rulers, and human weakness — the true cause of the collapse, of which World War III was only the visible consequence. He knows Kalki emerges in every cycle and seeks to identify and destroy the avatar before awakening; he wants permanent escape from the destruction that ends every age.
**Want / Fear / Wound.** *Want:* to make humanity kneel permanently and break the cycle that would unseat him. *Fear:* the avatar — the blue-white answer that makes corruption recoil; his whisper "...you." is recognition and alarm. *Wound:* he is entropy itself, and entropy is always ended by the turning of the age — he fights his own appointed expiry.
**Arc.** *Start:* serene, amused, omnipresent — he does not need to conquer; he taught the world to kneel. *Break open:* the moment the relic answers Kalki, Kali registers his enemy is awake and afraid. *End (saga-long):* shifts from amused manipulator to a presence at war, deferring the final reckoning ("...not yet.") as Raavan looms.
**Voice.** Whisper — layered, intimate, almost amused; imperatives that sound like temptation ("Take. Hoard. Survive.").
**Wardrobe / manifestation.** No body. Diseased gold, black-red rot, branching veins merging with architecture, broken dharma wheels, dice, dark thrones, propaganda banners, ritual smoke.
**Relationships.** Antagonist to Kalki across cycles; works through corrupt kings, warlords, and priests who kneel to the empty throne; precedes and is tied to the late-age threat of Raavan.

### KAKA / KAKABHUSHUNDI
**Role.** First guardian, guide, and central mystery; the one who raises Kalki.
**Brief.** On-screen / in-world, a realistic black crow — blue-black feather sheen, intelligent ancient eyes, still and watchful; never cute, never comedic.
**Key quote.** *(wordless — he leads, judges, and waits rather than explains.)*
**Background — behind-the-scenes canon.** Kaka **is Kakabhushundi**, a human in crow form — an ancient witness, sage, and keeper of knowledge. **Reveal timing:** his true identity stays hidden from Kalki and the audience through the **first half** and is revealed only at the **first-half reveal**, never before. *On-screen / in-world, until that reveal he reads only as an uncannily intelligent, ancient watchful crow — sometimes a normal bird, sometimes seeming to understand language, sometimes appearing before danger.*
**Want / Fear / Wound.** *Want:* to bring Kalki to the threshold of his destiny without handing him the truth. *Fear:* revealing too much too soon and producing mere programming instead of conscious choice. *Wound:* an ancient consciousness bound to watch and withhold across the turning of ages.
**Arc.** *Start:* silent caretaker of an infant. *Break open:* leads Kalki out of the sanctuary into history; later, his identity is revealed. *End:* the witness who knew all along, present at the awakening.
**Voice.** Indirect; reveals only what each stage requires; after the reveal, an ancient sage's measured fragments.
**Wardrobe.** Crow form (locked); human/Kakabhushundi aspect appears only at and after the reveal.
**Relationships.** Kalki's first parent and guide; ancient counterpart to the Old Sage and Unseen Mentor as a keeper of truth.

### VASUKI
**Role.** Guardian of Pinaka — the divine bow Kalki must claim.
**Brief.** A vast, ancient serpent; blue-black; eyes like cold stars; coiled around a weapon of light and ash in a cavern-shrine of living rock.
**Key quote.** *(wordless judgment.)*
**Background.** **Lord Shiva's serpent**, set to guard Pinaka since the first age. He recognizes the one destined to bear it.
**Want / Fear / Wound.** *Want:* to surrender the bow only to the rightful, tested bearer. *Fear:* the weapon falling to one who cannot carry the burden. *Wound:* an age-long, solitary vigil.
**Arc.** *Start:* immovable guardian. *Break open:* rises to test Kalki in long wordless judgment. *End:* the coils part; he yields Pinaka, having known him.
**Voice.** Silent presence; judgment by stillness and movement.
**Wardrobe.** Serpentine; cold-star eyes; Shiva-marks carved in the surrounding rock.
**Relationships.** Shiva's serpent; gatekeeper between Kalki and his weapon; tied to the cosmic function of destruction/renewal.

### RAAVAN
**Role.** Colossal demon-emperor; a late/final-age threat — **only a special ~2-second tease toward the end of the trailer.** Any larger role in the saga is deferred / undecided.
**Brief.** Giant battlefield scale; soot-black and diseased-gold armor; ancient crown; ember-red eyes; terrifying warlord presence. Many-headed nature only *suggested* by ghostly faces in smoke and shadow — never cluttered, never counted.
**Key quote.** *(silent — he only looks down.)*
**Background.** A returning colossal threat of major narrative significance, rising behind burning fortresses as armies scatter like ash below. *(Full storyline an open canon gap.)*
**Want / Fear / Wound.** *(Open gap — to be defined with the Raavan storyline.)*
**Arc.** *Start:* a flash on the horizon, ember eyes opening — the war beyond the war. *(Full arc TBD.)*
**Voice.** Wordless in the tease; one deep metallic hit accompanies his appearance.
**Wardrobe.** Soot-black and diseased-gold armor, ancient crown, ember eyes; smoke-suggested faces.
**Relationships.** Tied to Kali's age of collapse; the final-age threat after Kali ("...not yet.").

### THE OLD SAGE (Narrator)
**Role.** Prophetic narrator — the spine of the telling.
**Brief.** Unseen storyteller voice; ancient, grave, sorrowful, certain.
**Key quote.** *"And the prophecy asked of him the cruelest thing — to choose it."* / *"...so it was written. So it begins."*
**Background.** The voice that carries the prophecy across the saga, framing the age's forgetting, Kali's quiet conquest, and Kalki's burden.
**Voice.** Measured, biblical-mythic cadence; speaks of "it was written," truth sold, power prayed to, hope learning to make war.
**Relationships.** Frames the entire narrative; a keeper of the prophecy alongside Kaka. *(Whether the Old Sage and Kakabhushundi are the same voice is an open canon possibility.)*

### THE UNSEEN MENTOR (Dream Voice)
**Role.** The guiding voice of the dream/vision realm.
**Brief.** Disembodied; speaks within the cosmic dream sequences as Kalki walks the Pathways.
**Key quote.** *"Seven roads will open. Six you may walk. The last... you must become."* *(the trailer's teaser line — the saga canon now uses **six** dream sequences.)*
**Background.** One of the dream-realm guides who speak only in fragments, forcing Kalki to discover truth rather than be handed doctrine. The **Pathways are six cosmic dream sequences**; in each Kalki meets a god (some paired consorts) who helps him realize his destiny along the ladder Grace → Duty → Knowledge → Self-realisation → Perception → Acceptance, culminating in Vishnu (Acceptance). See `kaliyug_pathways.md`.
**Voice.** Cryptic, calm, riddling; gives instruction that doubles as prophecy.
**Relationships.** Guides Kalki across the dream realm; distinct from the Old Sage (waking prophecy) though both withhold deliberately.

### Supporting figures

- **Kalki's mother.** Bears the child through a virgin birth in the sanctuary and dies in childbirth, leaving only the infant and silence; no witnesses understand what has happened. Sacred but unexplained; the wound at the root of Kalki's isolation.
- **Divine male dream-form.** Appears only in the dream realm as a translucent, ethereal figure in **pinkish electric blue**; one of the guides drifting between the Pathways.
- **Divine female dream-form.** Appears only in the dream realm as a translucent, ethereal figure in **bluish electric pink**; counterpart guide among the Pathways.
- **The Pathway deities.** Across the **six dream sequences** Kalki meets Lakshmi, Ram, Saraswati **& Brahma**, Krishna, Shiva **& Parvati**, and Vishnu (the culmination) — in their **iconic traditional forms** (not the formless dream-form colour code), along the ladder Grace → Duty → Knowledge → Self-realisation → Perception → Acceptance. Full spreads + dream settings in `kaliyug_pathways.md`.
- **Kali's human agents.** Corrupt kings, warlords, and priests who kneel to the empty throne; the bazaar's debt-keepers, ration-hoarders, and blessing-sellers — the human surfaces through which systemic Kali acts. *(Named agents an open gap.)*
- **The saved (temple settlements).** Survivors who rise to fight — a wounded fighter lifting a salvaged spear, an old woman opening hidden food stores, a young girl pressing a green seed into cleared dark soil; the human stakes and the proof of renewal.

## 09 · Production Notes

**Budget tier.** AAA / premium — PS5 / Unreal-class semi-realistic cinematics; a flagship campaign with feature-scale ambition. Tentpole tier with franchise headroom (animated lore shorts, companion comics, mythic side-stories, a sequel in the restoration age).

**Format.** Game cinematic / interactive saga, 16:9, grounded dark mythic; cinematics rendered to ultra semi-realism.

**Length.** Full saga across the five-phase arc (Innocence → Entry → Search → Awakening → War); the 2-minute trailer is a marketing slice.

**Production challenges.**
- Holding the **grounded-mythic discipline**: the real world must stay physical and frightening while the dream realm carries all cosmic imagery — a hard, consistently policed line so the two never bleed.
- Sustaining the **gradual-revelation mystery** (Kalki's identity; Kaka's first-half reveal) so confirmation lands late enough to matter, early enough for transformation.
- Keeping **Kali bodiless and systemic** as a compelling, recurring antagonist without a face to fight, while never drifting into Goddess-Kali iconography.
- Rendering corruption as **infected tissue** fused into architecture at scale, and the seamless **smash-cut** between ruined world and pristine sanctuary.

**VFX requirements.** Red-black vein corruption crawling and recoiling across stone and metal; black ichor, glowing red cracks, blood-red storms and fog; volumetric smoke and ritual smoke; the dream realm's sacred geometry, turning mandalas, seven luminous gates, and translucent divine forms; the **transforming Pinaka** (bow↔spear↔blade of light and ash, never metal); the **Brahma Kavach** luminous armor-aura; the **Sharanga** spinning chakra-shield; the blue-white aura and the single unbroken thread of light; colossal **Raavan** with smoke-suggested faces; **Vasuki** at cavern scale.

**Casting direction.** Kalki — a young Indian male read of lean, alert, burdened intelligence; capable of stillness and analytical restraint, not a swaggering hero; able to carry grief at the awakening. Old Sage — a grave, ancient narrator voice with mythic cadence. Kali — a layered, intimate, almost-amused whisper, not a roaring villain. Kakabhushundi (post-reveal) — an ancient sage presence to match the crow's withheld gravity.

## Appendix · Locked Decisions & Open Canon Gaps

**Locked canon rulings (honor exactly, never contradict).**
- **Grounded mythic, never tech.** Kaliyug is NOT Vyom — never AI / cyberpunk / simulation / data-space / multiverse-as-system. The real world is post–WWIII Bharat: ash, rust, diseased gold, red-black vein corruption over ruined temples and ruined modern infrastructure.
- **Sacred geometry / cosmic mandalas / divine cosmic imagery appear ONLY in the dream/vision realm** — never in the grounded real world (no sky-mandalas, holograms, or luminous abstract symbols in the ruins).
- **Kali = the demon/personification of Kali Yuga** (corruption, vice, decay) — NEVER Goddess Kali, never divine feminine. Bodiless, systemic, asuric: empty throne, dice, red-black smoke-thought, whispers; he fears the avatar.
- **Kalki** = the unaware seeker who must CONSCIOUSLY choose the burden; awakens with blue-white aura + **Brahma Kavach** (luminous armor-AURA, not metal) + **Pinaka** (transforming bow/spear/blade of light and ash) + **Sharanga** (chakra-shield).
- **Kaka IS Kakabhushundi** — a human in crow form; identity is a MYSTERY through the first half, revealed only at the first-half reveal. In-world/on-screen he reads only as a watchful ancient crow until then.
- **Vasuki = Lord Shiva's serpent**, guardian of **Pinaka** (the bow Kalki claims).
- **The Pathways** are COSMIC DREAM SEQUENCES — **six** sequences; in each Kalki meets a god (some paired consorts) who helps him realize his destiny (Grace → Duty → Knowledge → Self-realisation → Perception → Acceptance), Vishnu the culmination. Never tech. Dream divine forms: male = pinkish electric blue, female = bluish electric pink (dream-only).
- **Trishula** — only Lord Shiva can truly wield it; conjured only by a being able to bear cosmic destructive force; NOT formed by combining weapons.
- **Raavan** = a colossal demon-emperor; in the trailer, **only a special ~2-second tease toward the end**; any larger saga role is undecided. Many-headed nature only suggested in smoke/shadow.
- **Time period** = post–World War III, the end of Kali Yuga.

**Genuine open canon gaps.**
- The **Pathways** — now **six dream sequences** with set deities + order (Lakshmi → Ram → Saraswati & Brahma → Krishna → Shiva & Parvati → Vishnu; see `kaliyug_pathways.md`); open only on whether each ties to a specific saga act/beat.
- The **Raavan storyline** — his full arc, want/fear/wound, relationship to Kali, and where the major confrontation falls.
- **Kali's complete origin** and his named human agents/factions.
- **Weapon-acquisition order** beyond Pinaka (when/whether Sharanga and Brahma Kavach are separately claimed).
- The **definitive ending** among the candidates (Kali destroyed and cycle resets / Kali contained under eternal vigilance / humanity left to choose / Kalki vanishes into legend).
- Detailed **timeline, quest structure, and full chapter breakdown**; supporting-cast roster.
- Whether the **Old Sage narrator and Kakabhushundi** are the same voice (left open).
