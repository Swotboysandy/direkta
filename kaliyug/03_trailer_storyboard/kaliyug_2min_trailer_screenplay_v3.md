# KALIYUG — 2-Minute AAA Cinematic Trailer (Screenplay v3)

Format: game cinematic trailer · 16:9 · Soulsborne-grade dark mythic (Bloodborne / Elden Ring register — monumental scale, sacred dread, decay-as-beauty). Re-cut from v2 to the project-wide **GAME ART DIRECTION** (`02_locked_visual_bible/kaliyug_game_art_direction.md`). World = **Indo-Gothic fusion** (Hindu sacred + colonial Indo-Saracenic Victorian-Gothic + prominent WWIII modern ruin, era-strata touching). Palette: desaturated soot/ash/rust ground, **one accent per frame** (crimson #8B1A1A OR blue-white #BFE3F2), diseased gold #9C7B2E as sickly rot, Blood Moon in the late beats. **Kali is abstract from the start** (empty throne / dice on a war-map / red-black smoke-that-moves-like-thought / layered whisper → Vishvarupa-scale cosmic vastness — never Goddess Kali). Sacred geometry / cosmic imagery ONLY in the Pathways dream beat. VO spine preserved exactly from v2.

---

## 1. BLACK SCREEN — 0:00–0:07

Out of the dark, one warm source: a single temple bell of cracked bronze sways through drifting ash, hung in the broken ribs of a CSMT-style pointed arch whose stained-glass is long gone. **Malignant ornament** — sinuous black-red veins that echo sacred carving gone wrong — creep across the bell's metal like blood finding its way into stone, the only colour in the frame. The bell is dwarfed; above it the vault is lost in red fog, scale unknowable. A faint, hair-thin **blue-white thread** of light flickers once across the black — then is swallowed. Sub-bass drone; one long, cracked bell strike.

OLD SAGE (V.O.)
It was written... that an age would forget it was ending.

## 2. EXT. RUINED METROPOLIS — DEAD SACRED RIVER — DAWN — 0:07–0:15

High, slow aerial drift over a monumental ruined city strung along a dead sacred river — a world that **dwarfs** everything in it. The **era-strata touch in one frame**: deepest, rock-cut temple stone and a curved Nagara **shikhara** still standing; over them a colonial **Indo-Saracenic** civic monument with ribbed vaults and **chhatri** cupolas; and **fused onto the older spires, prominent**, the WWIII modern ruin — snapped concrete flyovers, exposed rebar, dead war-machines — so the centuries seem grown into one corroded organism. Collapsed ghats, a half-sunk **gopuram** gateway, dead boats in black silt. Soot-grey ground; the only accent a thread of crimson rot bleeding along the riverline. Tops of the tallest spires vanish into red fog. Tiny survivors move like dust between the strata.

OLD SAGE (V.O.)
First, truth was sold. Then power was prayed to.

## 3. EXT. SACRED RIVER GHATS — DAY — 0:15–0:22

Stillness staged as dread. A mother kneels on worn temple steps, lowering cloth into the black water to wash it — and freezes as **red-black veins** pulse up through the dead current beneath her hands, the lone crimson accent in an ash-grey frame. Behind her, a **jali** lattice screen on a half-drowned shrine has been **clogged with the same veins, weeping ichor instead of light** through its holes. A child watches a dead fish drift past a defaced carved deity — its face overgrown, its meaning gone — while a small shrine-bell rings on its own, the one fragile sound in the silence.

OLD SAGE (V.O.)
The river that washed away sin... had nothing left to give.

## 4. EXT. RUINED HIGHWAYS — DAY — 0:22–0:30

A crane over a snapped concrete flyover — rebar splayed like torn sinew — collapsed **across** a toppled shikhara and a colonial spire, the modern ruin **prominent** on the sacred stone beneath. Scavengers cross the ash road, small against ribbed colonial vaulting that leans over them. A burned-out tank, half-buried, turned into a roadside shrine of bone and rust — offerings laid at its tracks, a single sodium-amber lamp guttering as the one warm source. Above, the spires climb into red fog and are lost. Far-off war drums begin, like a heart starting.

KALI (V.O. — whisper, layered, through radio static)
Take. Hoard. Survive. Survive.

## 5. EXT. DISEASED-GOLD BAZAAR — DAY — 0:30–0:39

A **rotten-opulent baroque** bazaar of gambling, debt, and hunger, lit in sickly **diseased gold #9C7B2E** — wealth rendered as disease, never warmth. Dice tumble across a blood-stained ledger. A corrupt guard drags a man into shadow under a cusped Indo-Gothic arch; a masked figure watches from a **chhatri** balcony. A priest sells painted blessings beside a locked ration cage where hands reach through the bars. A broken dharma-wheel sags on the wall, its carving overrun by **sinuous black-red veins** like ornament gone malignant — the single crimson note in the gold rot.

KALI (V.O. — whisper, almost amused)
Fear is a coin. Desire... is a leash.

## 6. INT. RUINED COURT — KALI'S EMPTY THRONE — 0:39–0:49

A corrupted **CSMT-style civic hall** — the dead state turned warlord court: pointed arches, ribbed vaults, gargoyles streaked with rust, the great dome above lost in red haze. At the false apex, beneath the dome, an **empty throne of black stone and diseased gold**. No body sits in it; **Kali is not a figure here, only the wrongness of the place.** Dice fall onto a war-map of Bharat spread across the worn steps. **Red-black smoke coils off the armrests and moves like thought** — sentient, unhurried. Corrupt kings, warlords, and priests kneel in silhouette before nothing. The throne stays empty. It does not need to be filled.

OLD SAGE (V.O.)
Kali did not need to conquer the world.

OLD SAGE (V.O.)
He taught it to kneel.

## 7. EXT. MOUNT KAILASH SANCTUARY — DAY — 0:49–0:58

Smash cut, and the noise stops. The high, cold sanctuary: a pure river over clean stones, pine, snow peaks under cold clear light — **beautiful but never cozy**, an uncanny stillness held above the rot. Cold river-cyan is the palette here; no crimson yet. Slow push-in to the water — a young face holds calm and unaware in the reflection, while behind it crimson smoke spreads and red-black veins crawl across the reflected sky, the corruption reaching even here as the lone accent. **KAKA** — a watchful ancient cosmic crow, blue-black sheen, eyes too old for a bird, never comedic — watches from a weathered, rune-worn shrine-stone.

OLD SAGE (V.O.)
But the prophecy hid one truth from him...

## 8. EXT. SANCTUARY EDGE — ASHLANDS — DAY — 0:58–1:07

KALKI stands at the seam where the sanctuary's clean green dies into the ashlands. Young Indian male, lean, alert, **burdened**; rough survival cloth, a torn shawl, ash grimed into his skin. No armour. No glow. No hero-light — a grounded seeker dwarfed by the dead ash plain ahead and the corrupted spires rising beyond it into red fog. Kaka beats his wings into the red storm-mist, then turns his head back, waiting. Kalki steps off the clean stone into the ash — descent, into deeper corruption.

OLD SAGE (V.O.)
...one child, raised beyond the corruption's reach—

## 9. INT. SHATTERED SHRINE — 1:07–1:15

A **garbhagriha** womb-sanctum of rock-cut stone, vein-choked and overgrown, its carved walls defaced. One warm source survives: a cracked **blue-white relic** half-fused to the wall — the bonfire of the frame. Kalki nears it. The relic pulses once, and the **malignant ornament recoils** — vein-tendrils peeling back across the floor and walls like burned tissue shrinking from a flame, the crimson accent fleeing the blue-white. Kalki freezes, staring at his own hand. Not triumphant. Afraid of what just answered him.

KALI (V.O. — whisper, sharp, alarmed — recognition)
...you.

## 10. DREAM REALM — THE PATHWAYS — 1:15–1:24

Kalki's eyes roll white and we fall out of the stone world into the **only place sacred geometry is permitted** — the dream realm. Deep indigo and cosmic black, abyssal and vast. He stands on a floating black-stone platform inside an impossible architecture of mandalas turning on different axes and slow astronomical light. Ahead, **SIX luminous gates** open in sequence — five blaze (blue-violet through magenta to white-gold, sacred geometry blooming clean and awe-terrible), **the sixth stays veiled** in folded dark, the cold orbital blue-black that the whole architecture quietly bends around — the road that cannot be walked, only become. Through the blazing gates, **reimagined-but-recognizable** deities resolve as cosmic-sublime presences, clean and numinous, no corruption upon them: through one, an **ash-ember Shiva-Parvati event-horizon** — a dark sun rimmed in fire fused with living-stone glow, an aperture of burning structural light that *sees*; through another, a **dawn-meridian Ram** cohering into a single perfect luminous axis, a bridge of light drawn clean across the dark, the one form that holds a solid line. Between the blazing gates drift two unnamed dream-forms — a divine MALE in pinkish electric blue, a divine FEMALE in bluish electric pink — translucent, ethereal. Kaka's shadow crosses the cosmic floor.

UNSEEN MENTOR (V.O.)
Six roads will open. Five you may walk.

UNSEEN MENTOR (V.O.)
The last... you must become.

## 11. EXT. ASHLANDS — THE BURDEN (CLOSE ON KALKI) — DAY — 1:24–1:31

Snap back to the grounded stone-and-rust world. Extreme close on Kalki's face — crimson rot-fire lighting one side, the thin **blue-white thread** on the other (the two accents split across one face, never blended). In his eyes flicker reflections: the poisoned river, the empty diseased-gold throne, starving crowds behind ration bars, the cold sanctuary river he left behind. Tears cut clean lines through the ash on his face. This is not a gift. It is a sentence — **hope is costly: grief, not glory.**

OLD SAGE (V.O.)
And the prophecy asked of him the cruelest thing — to choose it.

## 12. INT. CAVERN-SHRINE — VASUKI — 1:31–1:40

Descent into the chthonic dark: a drained **baoli** stepwell falling in inverted ziggurat tiers into a vast **rock-cut cavern-sanctum**, **Shiva-marks carved into the living rock** and glowing faintly. One shaft of cold light is the only mercy; dull red cracks held at bay at the edges. **VASUKI** — a vast cosmic serpent, blue-black, ancient, **eyes like cold stars** — uncoils from the dark, monumental, **dwarfing** Kalki, coiled around a weapon of light and ash: **PINAKA**. Kalki steps in, tiny before him. Stillness, a held breath — then the coils part in long, wordless judgment. Kalki does not flinch. His hand closes on PINAKA, which ripples from bow to spear to blade and back — **light and ash, never metal**. The cavern shudders.

KALKI
If it has to be carried... let it be me.

OLD SAGE (V.O.)
The serpent had guarded the bow since the first age. It knew him.

## 13. EXT. INFECTED BATTLEFIELD — DAY — 1:40–1:48

Cracked earth and infected temple ruins under the rising **Blood Moon (raktachandra)** — the crimson world-accent now total across the sky. Kalki drops to one knee as a **blue-white eruption** climbs out of the ground around him — the sacred counter-accent answering the red. **BRAHMA KAVACH** ignites over his body: a luminous protective **armour-aura**, light woven to his frame, **not a metal suit**. **SHARANGA** burns into being at his side as a spinning **chakra-shield**. Pinaka locks into a drawn bow of light-and-ash in his fist. The malignant veins rip backward across the battlefield, shrieking. Hope — but it costs him: his face is grief, not glory.

OLD SAGE (V.O.)
When the dark age set a crown upon its own head—

## 14. EXT. WAR FRONT — TEMPLE SETTLEMENTS — DAY — 1:48–1:56

Rapid, grounded mythic montage under the Blood Moon: survivors rise from **mandapa**-pillared temple settlements wedged into the era-strata ruin; a wounded fighter lifts a salvaged spear and charges; an old woman throws open hidden food stores; a young girl presses a saved green seed into cleared, dark soil and it takes — the one grief-lit beauty, an elegy not a comfort. Kalki moves through the war with controlled, burdened force, the **blue-white thread** trailing through soot and blood, driving toward a Kali-aligned host massing under red storm clouds beneath corrupted spires. Behind him, a single narrow road has been cleared of corruption — the thread made into a path.

OLD SAGE (V.O.)
—hope had to learn to make war.

## 15. EXT. RIDGE ABOVE ASH PLAIN — DUSK — 1:56–2:03

Kalki on a high ridge above a dead ash plain, **dwarfed** by the scale of the ruin. Behind him, the narrow cleared road glows faint **blue-white**, people following it. Ahead: infected siege armies, burning temples, **diseased-gold** banners to the horizon under the **Blood Moon** — the crimson sky pressing down. He raises Pinaka, now a drawn bow of pure light-and-ash against the red. Everything goes still — sacred dread staged in stillness. The full mix drops to wind and one low conch note.

KALKI
If this age must end...

KALKI
...let it end through me.

## 16. EXT. BURNING FORTRESS — RAAVAN (2-SECOND STING) — 2:03–2:05

A two-second flash, no warning. Behind a burning **Indo-Gothic** fortress whose spires fuse into snapped concrete, a colossus rises — **RAAVAN**. **Soot-black and diseased-gold**, an ancient crown, **ember-red eyes** opening in the dark. His many faces are only **suggested in the smoke and shadow** around his head, never counted. One vast hand grips the fortress wall; armies scatter like ash below, an entire war made small beneath him. He does not move. He only looks down — at us. One deep metallic hit.

KALI (V.O. — whisper, almost amused)
...not yet.

## 17. TITLE CARD — 2:05–2:09

Cut to black. Then **KALIYUG** carved into burned temple stone and twisted rebar-laced metal — the era-strata in the lettering itself — edges crusted with ash, **red-black smoke curling in from the corners**, and far above, a cosmic vastness pressing at the dark: not a face, the **age itself as the Old One**. Down the center runs a single hair-thin crack — and through it bleeds one faint, unbroken **blue-white thread** of light, the signature lifeline. A final conch hit decays into total silence, answered far away by the opening bell.

OLD SAGE (V.O.)
...so it was written. So it begins.

SUPER: KALIYUG

ONE FIX APPLIED (Beat 10), one audit error corrected. The audit declared "NO FIXES REQUIRED" and explicitly blessed the dream beat — but it endorsed a real internal contradiction. The draft simultaneously stated "the sixth stays veiled" AND rendered **cold-orbital Narayana / Vishnu coalescing through one of the blazing gates**. Per Pathways canon (`01_source_story_docs/kaliyug_pathways.md` §31, §67–71, §78) Vishnu *is* the sixth pathway — the culmination Kalki must *become*, not walk, the one that only blooms at the very end. Showing Vishnu resolved in a walkable gate contradicts the veiled-sixth logic in the same shot and soft-spoils the ending.

Fix: Vishnu is now held strictly behind the veiled sixth gate (rendered as "the cold orbital blue-black that the whole architecture quietly bends around... the road that cannot be walked, only become" — which also preserves canon's "Vishnu barely resolving from the still orbital dark" treatment, §6 / §69). The two deities resolving through the *blazing* gates are now both from the walkable five: **Shiva-Parvati** (Perception, #5 — unchanged, matches the §6 table verbatim) and **Ram** (Duty, #2 — rendered to his locked treatment, "a single perfect luminous axis / bridge of light... the only god who holds a clean, solid line," §6 table). Both reimagined-but-recognizable, clean/numinous, no corruption.

All other audit findings hold and were left intact:
- VO spine byte-preserved across all 17 beats (the six-gate line is verbatim against Pathways §78; only Beat 10 *visual prose* changed, no VO touched).
- Kali abstract throughout; Raavan distinct and named; unnamed pinkish-blue/bluish-pink dream-forms retained (color code reserved for unnamed forms only, Pathways §20 — verified).
- Sacred geometry quarantined to Beat 10; Indo-Gothic + prominent WWIII strata; one-accent discipline; blue-white thread seeded Beat 1 → paid off Beat 17; no Vyom/tech; Pinaka/Brahma Kavach/Sharanga all light-and-ash, never metal.
