# KALIYUG — 2-Minute AAA Cinematic Trailer (Screenplay v2)

Format: game cinematic trailer · 16:9 · grounded dark mythic (PS5 / Unreal ultra semi-realistic).
Trailer/montage format — timecoded scenes. VO spine = the Old Sage's prophecy; Kali whispers;
sparse Kalki / Unseen Mentor lines. Derived from `kaliyug_trailer_script_v2.md`.

---

## 1. BLACK SCREEN — 0:00–0:07

A single temple bell sways through drifting ash. Red-black veins creep across its cracked bronze like blood finding its way into stone. A faint blue-white line of light flickers once in the dark — then is swallowed. Sub-bass drone; one long, cracked bell strike.

OLD SAGE (V.O.)
It was written... that an age would forget it was ending.

## 2. EXT. RUINED METROPOLIS — DEAD SACRED RIVER — DAWN — 0:07–0:15

High, slow aerial drift over a ruined Indian metropolis strung along a dead sacred river: collapsed ghats, snapped bridges, shattered towers fused with temple spires, dead boats sunk in black silt. Smoke columns lean in the wind. Tiny survivors move like dust between stone and rusted metal.

OLD SAGE (V.O.)
First, truth was sold. Then power was prayed to.

## 3. EXT. SACRED RIVER GHATS — DAY — 0:15–0:22

A mother lowers cloth into the water to wash it and freezes as red veins pulse up through the black current beneath her hands. A child watches a dead fish drift past a half-drowned shrine, its little bell ringing on its own.

OLD SAGE (V.O.)
The river that washed away sin... had nothing left to give.

## 4. EXT. RUINED HIGHWAYS — DAY — 0:22–0:30

A crane over broken expressways fused with toppled temple spires and rusted war machines. Scavengers cross the ash road with bundles. A burned-out tank, half-buried, draped in cloth and turned into a roadside shrine — offerings of bone and rust laid at its tracks. Far-off war drums begin, like a heart starting.

KALI (V.O. — whisper, layered, through radio static)
Take. Hoard. Survive. Survive.

## 5. EXT. DISEASED-GOLD BAZAAR — DAY — 0:30–0:39

A bazaar of gambling, debt, and hunger. Dice tumble across a blood-stained ledger. A corrupt guard drags a man into shadow; a masked assassin watches from a balcony. A priest sells painted blessings beside a locked ration cage where hands reach through the bars. A broken dharma wheel sags on a wall, half-swallowed by red veins.

KALI (V.O. — whisper, almost amused)
Fear is a coin. Desire... is a leash.

## 6. INT. RUINED COURT — KALI'S EMPTY THRONE — 0:39–0:49

An empty throne of black stone and diseased gold. No body sits in it. Dice fall onto a war map of Bharat spread across the steps. Red-black smoke coils off the armrests and moves like thought. Corrupt kings, warlords, and priests kneel in silhouette. The throne stays empty. It does not need to be filled.

OLD SAGE (V.O.)
Kali did not need to conquer the world.

OLD SAGE (V.O.)
He taught it to kneel.

## 7. EXT. MOUNT KAILASH SANCTUARY — DAY — 0:49–0:58

Smash cut, and the noise stops. A pure river over clean stones, pine forest, snow peaks under cold clear light. Slow push-in to the water: a young face holds calm and unaware in the reflection — while behind it, crimson smoke spreads and red-black veins crawl across the reflected sky. KAKA, a serious, realistic black crow with blue-black sheen and ancient eyes, watches from a weathered shrine-stone.

OLD SAGE (V.O.)
But the prophecy hid one truth from him...

## 8. EXT. SANCTUARY EDGE — ASHLANDS — DAY — 0:58–1:07

KALKI stands at the seam where the sanctuary's clean green dies into the ashlands. Young Indian male, lean, alert, burdened; rough survival cloth, a torn shawl, ash grimed into his skin. No armor. No glow. Kaka beats his wings into the red storm-mist, then turns his head back, waiting. Kalki steps off the clean stone into the ash.

OLD SAGE (V.O.)
...one child, raised beyond the corruption's reach—

## 9. INT. SHATTERED SHRINE — 1:07–1:15

Vein-choked stone. Kalki nears a cracked relic half-fused to the wall. Blue-white light pulses once from the stone, and the corruption recoils — vein-tendrils peeling back across the floor and walls like burned tissue shrinking from a flame. Kalki freezes, staring at his own hand. Not triumphant. Afraid of what just answered him.

KALI (V.O. — whisper, sharp, alarmed — recognition)
...you.

## 10. DREAM REALM — THE PATHWAYS — 1:15–1:24

Kalki's eyes roll white and we fall into the dream realm. Deep indigo and cosmic black. He stands on a floating black-stone platform inside an impossible temple of sacred geometry and starlight. Ahead, SIX luminous gates open in sequence — blue-violet to magenta to white-gold light, mandalas turning in the void. A divine MALE form in pinkish electric blue and a divine FEMALE form in bluish electric pink drift between them, translucent and ethereal. Five pathways blaze; the sixth stays veiled. Kaka's shadow crosses the cosmic floor.

UNSEEN MENTOR (V.O.)
Six roads will open. Five you may walk.

UNSEEN MENTOR (V.O.)
The last... you must become.

## 11. EXT. ASHLANDS — THE BURDEN (CLOSE ON KALKI) — DAY — 1:24–1:31

Snap back to the real world. Extreme close on Kalki's face — red fire lighting one side, a thin blue-white line of light on the other. In his eyes flicker reflections: the poisoned river, the empty throne, starving crowds, the clean sanctuary river he left behind. Tears cut clean lines through the ash on his face. This is not a gift. It is a sentence.

OLD SAGE (V.O.)
And the prophecy asked of him the cruelest thing — to choose it.

## 12. INT. CAVERN-SHRINE — VASUKI — 1:31–1:40

A sacred cavern-shrine deep in living rock, carved with Shiva-marks, lit by one shaft of cold light and the dull glow of red cracks held at bay. VASUKI — Lord Shiva's great serpent, vast, blue-black, ancient, eyes like cold stars — coils around a weapon of light and ash: PINAKA. Kalki steps in, small before him. Vasuki rises, testing him in a long, wordless judgment. Kalki does not flinch. The coils part. Kalki's hand closes on PINAKA, which ripples from bow to spear to blade and back — light and ash, never metal. The cavern shudders.

KALKI
If it has to be carried... let it be me.

OLD SAGE (V.O.)
The serpent had guarded the bow since the first age. It knew him.

## 13. EXT. INFECTED BATTLEFIELD — DAY — 1:40–1:48

Cracked earth and infected temple ruins under a blood-red sky. Kalki drops to one knee as a blue-white eruption climbs out of the ground around him. BRAHMA KAVACH ignites over his body — a luminous protective armor-aura, light woven to his frame, not a metal suit. SHARANGA burns into being at his side as a spinning chakra-shield. Pinaka locks into a drawn bow of light in his fist. The red veins rip backward across the battlefield, shrieking. Hope, but it costs him — his face is grief, not glory.

OLD SAGE (V.O.)
When the dark age set a crown upon its own head—

## 14. EXT. WAR FRONT — TEMPLE SETTLEMENTS — DAY — 1:48–1:56

Rapid, grounded mythic montage: survivors rise from temple settlements; a wounded fighter lifts a salvaged spear and charges; an old woman throws open hidden food stores; a young girl presses a saved green seed into cleared, dark soil and it takes. Kalki moves through the war with controlled, burdened force, blue-white light trailing through soot and blood, driving toward a Kali-aligned host massing under red storm clouds. Behind him, a single narrow road has been cleared of corruption.

OLD SAGE (V.O.)
—hope had to learn to make war.

## 15. EXT. RIDGE ABOVE ASH PLAIN — DUSK — 1:56–2:03

Kalki on a high ridge above a dead ash plain. Behind him, the narrow cleared road glows faint blue-white, people following it. Ahead: infected siege armies, burning temples, diseased-gold banners to the horizon. He raises Pinaka, now a drawn bow of pure light against the red sky. Everything goes still. The full mix drops to wind and one low conch note.

KALKI
If this age must end...

KALKI
...let it end through me.

## 16. EXT. BURNING FORTRESS — RAAVAN (2-SECOND STING) — 2:03–2:05

A two-second flash, no warning. A colossal demon-emperor rises behind a burning fortress — RAAVAN. Soot-black and diseased-gold armor, an ancient crown, ember-red eyes opening in the dark. His many faces are only suggested in the smoke and shadow around his head, never counted. One vast hand grips the fortress wall; armies scatter like ash below. He does not move. He only looks down — at us. One deep metallic hit.

KALI (V.O. — whisper, almost amused)
...not yet.

## 17. TITLE CARD — 2:05–2:09

Cut to black. Then KALIYUG carved into burned stone and twisted metal, edges crusted with ash, red-black smoke curling in from the corners. Down the center runs a single hair-thin crack — and through it bleeds one faint, unbroken line of blue-white light. A final conch hit decays into total silence, answered far away by the opening bell.

OLD SAGE (V.O.)
...so it was written. So it begins.
