01 / 08 ESSENCE Reel One · Positioning & Voice

The Director's
Chair.

Direkta carries authority in its name. The brand must match — confident, direct, no decoration for decoration's sake. We elevate the creator's capability. We never gamify the craft.

A.Positioning Line
The director's chair for AI filmmaking.

One platform. Script to animatic. A full pre-production crew of nine specialised agents — and nothing ever ships without the Director's call.

B. Tagline
You direct.
AI delivers.
C. Operating Principle
Propose, don't commit.

Every agent shows options. The Director picks winners. Nothing saves without approval.

D.Personality — Five Traits
01
Confident
Speaks in declaratives. Never asks the user what they meant when it can ask one sharp question instead.
02
Precise
Filmmaker vocabulary, never generic. A "Cinematographer," not an "image generator." A "beat," not a "step."
03
Cinematic
Dark, wide, composed. The product should feel like walking onto a quiet, well-lit set.
04
Direct
No fluff. No hedging. No upsell. Verbs first. The Director's time is the most expensive resource on set.
05
Crafted
Every detail considered. Direkta is for people who treat filmmaking as a craft — the product treats itself the same way.
E.Voice Rules — Five Non-Negotiables

If a sentence doesn't pass all five, rewrite it.

R—01 Speak Set Filmmaking vocabulary by default. "Roll. Cut. Hold for picture. Slate." Generic tech words are a last resort.
R—02 Verbs First Lead with the action. "Direct a Cinematographer." Not "Image generation tool."
R—03 Propose, Don't Decide The agent surfaces options. The Director picks. "Here are six readings. Which one stays?"
R—04 No Hype No "magic," no "revolutionary," no "unleash." We're a workplace, not a sales floor.
R—05 Respect the Craft Never reduce filmmaking decisions to clicks. "Cast your lead" beats "Set up your character."

03 / 08 COLOUR Reel Three · Dark Palette · One Accent

Dark first.
One accent.

The base is film-negative black. The light is warm cinema white, never sterile. The single accent is tungsten — the colour of a studio lamp, a film leader countdown, an exposed frame. There is no second accent. There are no gradients.

Default canvas, app background, dark mode primary. Almost-black, never pure — film grain lives in the lift.

B.Proportion — 70 / 20 / 10
Ink — 70% Bone — 20% Accent — 10%

If the accent exceeds 10% of any frame, something is wrong. It must remain rare to remain commanding.

Three Rules

01. No gradients. Not on backgrounds, not on type, not on buttons. The palette is flat or it is not Direkta.

02. No purple. No teal. No second accent. The tungsten stands alone.

03. Bone over white. Pure white reads as printer paper, not film stock. Bone is the standard.

05 / 08 ICONS Reel Five · Five Workspaces

Drawn like
a set plan.

Icons are linear, monoline, square-cornered. Each workspace gets one defining shape — the script page, the framed silhouette, the storyboard grid, the node graph, the export frame. Geometric, not illustrated. Honest, not cute.

WS — 01
Screenplay
Where the story lives.
The script page with a scene heading bar and indented dialogue. A written document, never a paper-clip metaphor.
WS — 02
Casting
Soul ID · Visual identity.
A silhouette inside film-frame brackets. The brackets are the frame; the figure is who lives inside it. Never a smiling person.
WS — 03
Storyboard
Beat row · Variants picked.
A grid of frames with one filled — the picked winner. The right-most column is shorter, suggesting the next beat coming.
WS — 04
Stitch
Node graph · Animatic.
Three nodes, two directional connections. The metaphor is the editing suite — frames as nodes, clips as the lines between.
WS — 05
Export
Frame · Deliverable out.
A film frame with content moving down to a baseplate. Delivery — to disk, to pitch, to client. Never a cloud arrow.

RULE 01

1.5 px stroke @ 24 px

Every icon built on a 24-unit grid with a constant 1.5-unit stroke. Bumps to 2px at 16px for legibility. Scale the icon, never the stroke ratio.

RULE 02

Hard corners only

stroke-linecap and stroke-linejoin: miter. No rounded ends. No soft joins. Cameras don't round.

RULE 03

One fill per icon, max

If an icon must communicate state — selected, active, generated — fill exactly one shape inside it. Two fills is too loud.

06 / 08 MOTION Reel Six · Three Primitives

Shutter.
Cut. Rack focus.

Three motion primitives, all borrowed from a camera. Nothing floats. Nothing morphs. Nothing bounces. Hover any frame below to play.

M — 01

Shutter

180 ms · cubic-bezier(.55, 0, .1, 1)

For decisive appearance / disappearance. Modals open. Panels close. Renders finish. Top and bottom converge on centre — like a mechanical shutter dropping.

Hover frame to play→

B
M — 02

Cut

0 ms · instant

For navigation, tab changes, beat-to-beat. No fade. No slide. No crossfade. The next state replaces the current in a single frame. The most filmic transition there is.

Hover frame to cut→
Context
Focus
M — 03

Rack Focus

480 ms · ease-out

When attention shifts within a frame — tooltip activates, agent is speaking, selection committing. Background blurs, foreground sharpens. The reverse on release.

Hover frame to pull→
Forbidden Motion
No Float

Cards do not bob, hover-lift, or wiggle. They are placed on the desk.

No Morph

Shapes never tween into other shapes. A square that becomes a circle is a magic trick. We are not magicians.

No Bounce

Springy easing curves belong on consumer apps. Direkta is set-quiet.

No Confetti

No celebration animations. The work is the reward. A finished render is a fact, not a party.
